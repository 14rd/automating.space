3. Interpretation and Definitions

  • Personal data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • Data processing: Any operation performed on personal data, such as collection, storage, modification, transfer, deletion, etc.
  • Data Controller: The business named in this policy, which determines the purposes and means of data processing.
  • Data Processor: A natural or legal person that processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller.
  • Data Subject: Any identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data we process.
  • Cookies: Small data files placed and stored on your device by the web browser.
  • Consent: The voluntary, specific, informed and unambiguous consent of the data subject to the processing of personal data.
  • Data breach: A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data.

5. Purposes and Legal Bases of Data Processing

Data processing always has a legal basis under the GDPR.

5.1. Performance of a Contract — Article 6(1)(b) GDPR

  • Creating and managing an account and providing access
  • Responding to customer service requests

5.2. Legitimate Interest — Article 6(1)(f) GDPR

  • Maintaining website security and preventing abuse
  • Preparing aggregated statistics and analyses for service development
  • Enforcing and defending legal claims

5.3. Consent — Article 6(1)(a) GDPR

  • Placing non-essential cookies, for example marketing and analytics cookies

5.4. Compliance with a Legal Obligation — Article 6(1)(c) GDPR

  • Fulfilling accounting and tax obligations
  • Complying with requests from authorities

6. Data Retention Periods

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to achieve the purpose of processing, or as required by law:

  • Contact messages: For 1 year from the last contact
  • Newsletter subscription data: Until unsubscribing
  • Web server logs: For a maximum of 90 days, unless required for investigating a security incident

7. Data Security

In order to protect your data, we apply technical and organisational measures that comply with industry standards, in particular:

  • Use of SSL/TLS encryption during data transmission (HTTPS protocol)
  • Passwords are stored using one-way encryption (hashing)
  • Access control: only authorised employees have access to personal data
  • Regular backups and software updates
  • In the event of a data breach, compliance with the GDPR's 72-hour notification obligation to the Hungarian National Authority for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (NAIH)

11. Your Rights

11.1. Right of Access — Article 15 GDPR

You have the right to request information on whether we process your personal data, and if so, to receive a copy and related information.

11.2. Right to Rectification — Article 16 GDPR

You may request the immediate correction or completion of inaccurate or incomplete data.

11.3. Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten") — Article 17 GDPR

If certain conditions are met, you may request the deletion of your data. This right does not apply if processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation.

11.4. Right to Restriction of Processing — Article 18 GDPR

You may request restriction of processing, for example if you dispute the accuracy of the data.

11.5. Right to Data Portability — Article 20 GDPR

In the case of automated data processing based on consent or a contract, you have the right to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (e.g. JSON or CSV).

11.6. Right to Object — Article 21 GDPR

You may object at any time to processing based on legitimate interest. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

11.7. Automated Decision-Making — Article 22 GDPR

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing if it has legal or similarly significant effects.

11.8. Withdrawal of Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of prior processing.
